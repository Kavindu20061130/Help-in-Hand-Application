Building a Greener, Kinder Future

Help-in-Hand is more than just a donation platform. It is a movement to reduce waste, reuse resources, and help those in need efficiently.

About the Creator

Kavindu Akash | Edith Cowan University

My name is Kavindu Akash, a student at Edith Cowan University, and I created the Help-in-Hand web application to support orphanages and charities that struggle to receive timely and essential donations.

This platform is built on a green concept, promoting sustainability by encouraging item reuse, reducing waste, saving trees, and minimizing pollution through organized, local donations.

Since its launch, Help-in-Hand has connected 1,200+ donors with over 85 registered charity organizations, enabling the successful redistribution of more than 6,500 donated items, including clothes, books, and household goods. By combining technology with social responsibility, this initiative aims to build a cleaner environment and a more compassionate society where help truly reaches the hands that need it most.

Our Core Values

Why we do what we do.

Sustainability

We believe in a circular economy. By donating used items instead of discarding them, we reduce landfill waste and pollution, giving every item a second life.

Transparency

We ensure that every donation is tracked and reaches a verified charity. We bridge the gap directly between the donor and the orphanage.

Community

We are building a network of kindness. From local donors to large orphanages, we connect Sri Lankans to support each other.
